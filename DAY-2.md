## Section 1: Explanation — What is Prompt Engineering?

**What it is**

When you talk to an AI like Claude, you type a message — that message is called a *prompt*. Prompt engineering is simply the skill of writing those messages in a smarter, clearer way so the AI gives you better, more useful answers.

Think of it like giving directions. "Go somewhere near the market" will get you a confused driver. "Turn left at the red gate, go 200m, stop at the blue shop" gets you exactly where you want. Same logic applies to AI.

**Why it matters**

AI tools like Claude are incredibly powerful — but they can only work with what you give them. A fuzzy question gets a fuzzy answer. A well-crafted prompt gets a focused, actionable, high-quality response. Prompt engineering is the difference between using AI as a toy and using it as a professional tool.

---

## Section 2: Weak vs Improved Prompt Example

**Weak prompt:**
> *"Tell me about marketing."*

What's wrong: Too broad. No context. The AI doesn't know if you want a definition, a strategy, examples, a book summary, or something else. You'll get a generic paragraph that helps nobody.

**Improved prompt:**
> *"I run a small bakery in my city. Write me 3 short Instagram captions (under 15 words each) to promote my new mango cake. Keep the tone warm and fun."*

What's better: It tells the AI *who you are*, *what you need*, *the format*, *the length*, and *the tone*. The output will be ready to use immediately.

**Three practical benefits of better prompts:**

1. **Save time** — You get a usable answer on the first try instead of going back and forth five times asking the AI to "fix it."

2. **Get professional-quality output** — Specific prompts unlock the AI's real capability. You stop getting generic text and start getting tailored, expert-level responses.

3. **Stay in control** — You decide the format, tone, length, and focus. Better prompts mean the AI works *for* you, not the other way around.
